Professional Experience

I started my career as a Junior Java Programmer in a startup about 20 years ago. This job gave me a good foundation in Java programming. We were building a product for the garment industry and doing all the coding using EditPlus.

After working at the start up for 18 months, I got the opportunity to work as a project Assistance at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), a famous and one of the top educational and research institutes of the world. I was building an editor for Kannada (a South Indian Language), managing websites and other solutions as part of a Government program to promote Indian Languages in Information technology.

My first big break came in May 2004, at a software consulting startup, Iteamic. It was this company that changed my future. I worked for this company for 9 years in different roles and got an opportunity to work for fortune 500 companies. In 2007, I was assigned to a strategic account of Iteamic, Graduate School of Business, Stanford University. In 2012, I moved to the US to work as Onsite Delivery Manager for Ciber(acquired Iteamic in 2008) and later in 2013 joined Stanford University as a Software Architect/Project Manager.

Over the last 20 years of professional experience, I have held several roles across different organizations.

I have primarily been a Java programmer building enterprise applications using Spring and Hibernate. However, over the years, I have also added PHP, Drupal, and Salesforce into my technical basket. I have also worked on RDBMS. Refer to my detailed resume here.
